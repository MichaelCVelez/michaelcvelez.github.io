Honing my Skills

By Michael • January 8, 2021
⚲ Austin, TX
Scroll to Bottom

Samsung Austin Semiconductor

My journey at Samsung has been an incredibly unique one. This post is particularly special because my employment is still ongoing at the time of this writing. As you may recall, I am employed by a contracting agency known as Randstad and was assigned to Blizzard Entertainment for a customer service role. I enjoyed the job and environment but knew I needed to utilize my degree and improve my skills before I began to lose them. Samsung just happen to be another client of Randstad which made my transition much easier.

Originally, I applied to Samsung for a manufacturing role in the factory which I would later find out was one of two "Fabs" which operate at the Austin plant. Imagined that I would be side by side in a long line of workers, assembling pieces on a conveyor belt and might be able to work my way up eventually. Luck was on my side however. The onsite Randstad recruiter found a unique position which favored my Computer Science background! It was not long before I met my supervisor and future teammates. My nerves very quickly melted away at the interview and I was hired immediately! My excitement was overwhelming. I would finally be in a developer role at a Fortune 100 company in Austin, TX!

I joined the Smart Auto Defect Classification (SADC) team of the Defect Engineering department. I quickly learned our objective was to minimize the risk of and eliminate potential defects in the long line of semiconductors produced in the fabs daily. SADC uses machine learning in order to identify defects on the wafers which helps technicians in categorizing and resolving them. This was my first exposure to machine learning on a deep level, which was discouraging initially. I was essentially the least knowledgeable at ML but my strengths quickly began to shine through.

My early assignments included automating common tasks before growing into larger projects spanning multiple quarters. I evolved into the primary developer and owner of DE-Systems Visual Object Tagging Tool (VoTT), an annotation and labeling tool used for masking defects across thousands of images which is primarily coded in Typescript/Javascript and utilizes the React + Redux JS libraries. The project began with open source code which I extended to add multiple user support and integrated database connectivity through RESTful API communication. My supervisor specifically requested the ability to track user action history and daily dynamic assignment delivery.

I quickly learned that I excel most in front end development, which I credit to my keen eye for intuitive user interface design. I greatly enjoyed taking the liberty of improving VoTT's UI and releasing periodic quality of life patches to improve our users' experience! Some of my work aimed at streamlining the application and reducing overall repetitive mouse movements, which was well received by one user who suffers from Carpal Tunnel syndrome. These updates earned enough recognition that I was later awarded a Safety Champion award for my department!

Fast forward 2 years and as my team began to grow, our collective understanding of the software development life cycle and best practices did as well! I started on a 2-3 man team focused on machine learning and am now part of a team as big as 7-8 members with a diverse skillset including full-stack development, database management AND machine learning! My my most recent projects have been focused on Laravel web development and centralizing all of our projects onto a single website that is accessible to our customer (our neighboring Defect Control department).

I am proud of my growth, having started as a developer with primarily Java, C and SQL experience and now becoming proficient in Javascript, PHP and Python as well! This chapter is still being written and despite the hardships that the world as a whole is enduring at this time, I believe the best is still yet to come!
