Humble Beginnings

By Michael • August 20, 2020
⚲ El Paso, TX
Scroll to Bottom

Community Scholars Inc.

One of the first times I set foot in my current home of Austin, TX was actually when I was an intern at El Paso’s Community Scholars Inc. In 2011, I was selected amongst a handful of my peers to travel to the state capitol and present my research on how quality of life has changed in El Paso from 1990-2010. This was my introduction to the world of public policy, local and state government. Although these subjects once bored me, ironically enough, they are always at the forefront of my mind due to today’s political climate.

During my time at Community Scholars (C.S.), I scheduled and conducted professional interviews with El Paso County district representatives and state officials to gather testimonies for my research. The connections and friendships during this time helped me to develop a deep appreciation for the city I grew up in. Prior to CS, I believed in the common saying “There’s nothing to do in El Paso.” This wasn’t in reference to job opportunities but was often used to complain about the lack of recreational activities.

During my 50+ hours of community service, I quickly learned there’s plenty to do in the El Paso area. I had no idea there was a plaza theater and a Classic Film Festival every summer! I had never heard of the Eagle in the Sun Triathlon or the niche restaurant called The Garden in the trendy entertainment district downtown. My world was starting to open up as discovering local artists, politicians and fine Mexican cuisine filled my workdays.

I am still connected with many of the colleagues I had the pleasure of working with. It makes me happy to see everyone growing up and developing their own families and careers in some far off city, many miles away. It warms my heart knowing that we all started as the geeky interns stuffed into ill fitting suits in the killer El Paso heat. If there’s one thing I always knew, it was that I would return to live in Austin.
